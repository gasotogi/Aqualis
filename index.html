About the project
Aqualis

The "Aqualis" app is an innovative tool designed to raise awareness about marine life and the importance of ocean preservation. It provides features for monitoring water quality, promoting the conservation of endangered species, and delivering educational information about the underwater world. In summary, Aqualis serves as a digital window to the underwater universe, encouraging ocean protection and understanding.

About the App advancements

This project falls under the theme: "What's up with this water?" and aims to address the need for an app that allows users to check the water quality in their local water bodies, identify endangered species inhabiting these waters, and learn how to protect them. Additionally, the app aims to provide information about the origin of water from a specific location, helping users understand the relationship between water quality and local ecology. Simplicity of use and accessibility were top priorities in developing this app. Incorporating the principles of inclusive design, as defined by Marcelo Sales, a Designer specializing in digital accessibility and inclusive design, this tool has been designed and planned specifically to ensure the best user experience and inclusion of the widest audience (both neurotypical and atypical individuals included).
